    E-invoicing

    France's 2026-2027 reform, in plain terms.

    From 1 September 2026, France moves to mandatory electronic invoicing between businesses. Here is what changes, when, and for whom — and what COSMO handles for you.

    What it is

    A structured invoice, not a PDF by email

    An electronic invoice isn't a PDF emailed to your client. It's a structured file — Factur-X, UBL or CII, following the European EN 16931 standard — routed through a state-approved platform (PA), the mandatory intermediary between you and your customer. A plain PDF will no longer count as a valid business-to-business invoice.

    The official timeline

    2026

    Receiving — every company

    From 1 September 2026, every VAT-registered company in France must be able to receive electronic invoices — no size exception. Even if you don't issue them yet, your suppliers will send you some.

    2026

    Issuing — large companies & mid-caps

    On the same date, large companies (over 5,000 employees) and mid-caps / ETI (250 to 5,000 employees) must also issue all their B2B invoices electronically.

    2027

    Issuing — small businesses & the self-employed

    On 1 September 2027, the obligation to issue extends to everyone else: SMEs, micro-businesses and the self-employed. One extra year to issue — but not to receive.

    Who's concerned

    Every VAT-registered business in France

    Whatever your size, revenue, legal form or tax regime. Even micro-entrepreneurs under the VAT franchise are in scope, for both receiving and issuing. The reform covers domestic B2B — between two French businesses. Sales to consumers (B2C) and cross-border trade fall under e-reporting; the public sector (B2G) stays on Chorus Pro.

    If you do nothing

    €15 per non-compliant invoice

    Failing to issue in electronic format exposes you to €15 per invoice (capped at €15,000/year), and €250 per missing e-reporting transmission (capped at €45,000/year). A grace period applies at launch — but the obligation is real.

    Frequently asked questions

    What you might be wondering

    Is this really compliant with French regulations?

    Yes. COSMO keeps your books under the French chart of accounts (PCG), calculates VAT and produces an FEC export in the format expected by the French tax authority (DGFiP). Invoices are generated in Factur-X format (EN 16931 standard). COSMO structures and checks your accounts; filing your returns remains your responsibility.

    Do I still need an accountant?

    For a micro-entreprise, a freelancer or a small association, an accountant isn't required: COSMO lets you keep your own books. If you work with an accountant, you can hand them your FEC and your file in a single export. COSMO is an assistance tool, not legal or tax advice.

    Is e-invoicing (Factur-X) included?

    Yes — generating EN 16931-compliant Factur-X invoices is included, with a 21-rule check. Issuing and receiving via our approved-platform partner make you compliant with the e-invoicing reform, without changing how you work.

    Is my data secure?

    Your data is hosted in the European Union, encrypted in transit and isolated by organization. You remain the owner of your data and can export it at any time. Details are in our privacy policy (French — the governing version).

    Can I cancel easily?

    For freelancers and micro-entrepreneurs, but also small & mid-sized companies on the standard regime — SAS, SASU, SARL/EURL — and associations, in France, Italy and Spain, in one tool.
